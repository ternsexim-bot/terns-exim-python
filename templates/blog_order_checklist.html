Fastener Export Order Checklist: What to Prepare Before You Buy

How-To Guide 23 June 2026 7 min read Procurement · Export Process
In this article:
  1. Why a complete specification matters
  2. Technical specification checklist
  3. Commercial specification checklist
  4. Certification and documentation requirements
  5. Common specification mistakes and how to avoid them
  6. How the TERNS EXIM RFQ process works

The most common reason a fastener export quotation takes longer than it should — or comes back inaccurate — is an incomplete specification from the buyer. When a supplier has to make assumptions about grade, finish, Incoterm, or certification, the quotation they return may not be comparable to others, and the order that eventually ships may not be exactly what the project requires. This checklist is designed to help you prepare everything before you send your first enquiry.

Why a Complete Specification Matters

Industrial fasteners are not commodities with a single specification. An M20 bolt can be:

  • Grade 4.6, 8.8, 10.9, or 12.9 — varying 3× in strength
  • Plain, zinc-plated, hot-dip galvanized, or Geomet-coated — varying 40× in corrosion life
  • ISO 4014, DIN 933, ASTM A307, or ASTM A193 B7 — different thread forms, mechanical requirements
  • Supplied with or without an EN 10204 3.1 MTC

Each of these variables affects price, lead time, and fitness for purpose. A quotation that assumes grade 8.8 when you need 10.9, or plain finish when you need HDG, is not useful. Providing a complete specification upfront eliminates these assumptions and results in a quotation you can act on immediately.

Technical Specification Checklist

For each line item in your enquiry, confirm you have specified:

  • Product type — hex bolt, socket cap screw (SHCS), stud bolt, anchor bolt, carriage bolt, U-bolt, threaded rod, or other. If it is a nut or washer, specify the matching standard.
  • Applicable standard — ISO (e.g. ISO 4014, ISO 4032), DIN (e.g. DIN 933, DIN 934), ASTM (e.g. ASTM A193 B7, ASTM A325), BS, or other. The standard defines the dimensional tolerances, thread form, and mechanical test method.
  • Nominal diameter — metric (M8, M12, M20, etc.) or imperial (1/2 inch, 3/4 inch, etc.). For ASTM standards, also specify thread pitch/UNC or UNF.
  • Nominal length — the bolt length from underside of head to end of shank. For partially threaded bolts (ISO 4014), also confirm the required thread length if it differs from standard.
  • Thread pitch — coarse (standard, assumed unless stated) or fine. Specify if fine pitch is required, along with the pitch value (e.g. M16 × 1.5).
  • Property class / grade — e.g. 8.8, 10.9, 12.9, A2-70, A4-80, B7, L7, etc. This is non-negotiable — never leave it to supplier assumption.
  • Material — carbon steel, low-alloy steel, stainless steel 304/316, duplex, monel, etc. For ASTM standards, specify the ASTM grade directly (A193 B7 = Cr-Mo alloy; A307 Grade A = plain carbon).
  • Surface finish / coating — plain/black, zinc electroplated (specify microns if known), hot-dip galvanized (ISO 10684), Geomet, Delta Tone, phosphate and oil, or other. Note: HDG is not compatible with 10.9 or 12.9.
  • Quantity — in pieces (pcs) or metric tonnes (MT). For mixed sizes, list each line item separately with its own quantity.
  • Associated hardware — matching nuts (ISO 4032, ISO 7042 prevailing torque, etc.), washers (ISO 7089 flat, ISO 7090 chamfered, hardened F436, etc.). Specify grade and finish to match bolts.

Commercial Specification Checklist

  • Destination port and country — e.g. "Port of Rotterdam, Netherlands" or "Jebel Ali, UAE". The destination country also determines if any import approvals or documentation are required.
  • Incoterm — FOB (buyer handles freight and insurance from Indian port), CIF (supplier includes cost, insurance, freight to destination port), CFR, DAP, or other. See our detailed guide on FOB vs CIF for fastener imports. Quoting without a stated Incoterm is ambiguous and prevents meaningful price comparison across suppliers.
  • Payment terms — TT (telegraphic transfer) in advance, TT against copy BL, LC at sight, LC 30/60/90 days, or documentary collection. Mention your preferred terms so the supplier can factor in financing cost.
  • Required shipment date or lead time — "ex-works by 15 August 2026" or "shipment within 6 weeks of order confirmation." Tight timelines affect which manufacturers can supply and may attract a premium. Always state if the date is firm or indicative.
  • Packaging requirements — standard export packing (wooden crates or pallets in cartons) is assumed unless otherwise stated. Specify if you require specific carton weights, inner bag quantities, pallet dimensions, or container loading instructions.

Certification and Documentation Requirements

Specify the following before quoting — adding certification requirements after order confirmation can delay or block shipment:

  • Mill Test Certificate type — EN 10204 3.1 (standard), EN 10204 3.2 (third-party witnessed), or EN 10204 2.2 (where your spec permits). TERNS EXIM provides 3.1 as standard on all orders.
  • Third-party inspection — pre-shipment inspection (PSI) by SGS, Bureau Veritas, Intertek, or TÜV. Specify stage (ex-works, at port) and scope (visual, dimensional, hardness, tensile).
  • Country-specific approvals — CE marking (for anchors under CPR), SASO (Saudi Arabia), ESMA (UAE), or other national marks. These require advance planning and may add lead time.
  • Export documents required — commercial invoice, packing list, certificate of origin (COO), phytosanitary certificate (for wooden crates), insurance certificate. Standard export documents are provided; COO and additional certificates should be requested upfront. See our export process page for a full list of documents TERNS EXIM provides.

Common Specification Mistakes and How to Avoid Them

Mistake Consequence How to Avoid
Not specifying property class Supplier quotes lowest (cheapest) grade; may not meet structural requirement Always state grade explicitly: 8.8, 10.9, A2-70, B7, etc.
Specifying HDG finish for 10.9 bolts Technically incorrect; hydrogen embrittlement risk; supplier may silently substitute Use Geomet or mechanical zinc for 10.9/12.9 — see our bolt grades guide
Quoting quantity in KG instead of pieces Unit price comparison is inaccurate; bolts may arrive in non-standard lengths Always quote in pieces (pcs). Add KG as reference only.
No Incoterm stated Prices from different suppliers are not comparable; risk and cost split is ambiguous State Incoterm in every enquiry. Use FOB as default for experienced importers.
Not requesting MTC upfront Supplier ships without MTC; retrospective documentation is difficult or impossible Include "EN 10204 3.1 MTC required" in every enquiry and purchase order.
