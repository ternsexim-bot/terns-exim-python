Product Variants

Types of Threaded Rods We Export

Full-thread rods to stud bolts for flanges — covering standard civil, structural, and high-pressure industrial applications.

Full Thread Rod

DIN 975. Thread along entire length. Cut to size on site. Standard for HVAC, electrical support, and general assembly.

Double-End Stud

DIN 976 / ASTM A193. Threaded both ends with a plain shank. Used for flange bolting and through-bolted connections.

Tap-End Stud

Short threaded end screws into a tapped hole; longer end accepts a nut. For cylinder heads, pump bodies, and housings.

Anchor Rod

Plain shank with threaded top for leveling nuts and nut-above-plate. Standard in equipment and machinery foundations.

High-Temp Stud Bolt (B7)

ASTM A193 Grade B7 (Cr-Mo). For pressure vessel, boiler, and pipeline flange applications under elevated temperature and pressure.

Tie Rod Assembly

Threaded rod with coupling nuts and end plates. For structural tension members in bridges, formwork, and precast concrete.

Buyer FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions — Threaded Rods

What is the difference between a threaded rod and a stud bolt?

A threaded rod (DIN 975) is fully threaded along its entire length, while a stud bolt (DIN 976) has threading at both ends with an unthreaded middle section.

What length are your threaded rods supplied in?

Standard length is 1 metre; custom lengths are available on request.

What is the minimum order quantity for threaded rods?

Our MOQ is 10,000 pieces or 700 kg depending on size.
